Search syntax

Basics

budgetFind a single word.
budget reportBoth words required (space means AND).
"purchase order"An exact phrase, words in this order.

Boolean & exclusion

invoice AND 2025Both terms must appear.
cat OR dogEither term may appear.
report -draftHas report, excludes draft.
report NOT draftSame as -draft.
a b OR cGroups as (a AND b) OR c.

Wildcards

invoic** = any run of characters (matches invoice, invoices…).
organi?e? = exactly one character (organise, organize).

Wildcards match within a single word, not across spaces.

Regex /…/

/inv.*ce/A raw regex between slashes.
/colou?r/Matches color and colour.
/error\d+/\d \w . * + ? | {n,m} and char classes work.

Matches whole words (case-insensitive), anchored end to end — so /inv/ matches only the exact word “inv”; use /inv.*/ for “starts with inv”. A pattern can’t span spaces; for multi-word matching use a "phrase". No look-around or backreferences.

Fuzzy & proximity

paymnet~1Allow 1 typo (edit distance, max 2).
color~Bare ~ defaults to distance 1.
"quick fox"~5The two words within 5 of each other.

Combining

invoic* AND 2025 -voidMix any of the above freely.
/rece?ipt/ OR "purchase order"Regex or a phrase.

Note: snippets highlight matched terms for word/phrase searches; wildcard, regex, and fuzzy results show the start of the file.
